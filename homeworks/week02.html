Dataset Creation & Distributions

A dataset is a structured collection of related data. It represents the raw information gathered for analysis or modeling. A distribution, on the other hand, describes how the values of a variable in that dataset are spread or occur, showing the frequency or probability of different outcomes. When analyzing one variable at a time, it’s called a univariate distribution (e.g., the distribution of heights in a group). When analyzing two or more variables together, it’s called a multivariate distribution (e.g., the joint distribution of height and weight).
